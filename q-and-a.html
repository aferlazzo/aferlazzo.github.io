Hi, I'm Tony Ferlazzo, Front-end Web Developer

Everything you want to know but were afraid to ask

My BFFs are JavaScript, HTML, and CSS, and I've been successful with both large and small companies. I've also written Chrome Extensions as required, have dabbled with canvas, have utilized sprites and compressed images as deemed appropriate. I've gzipped and minimized. I've learned hacks to circumvent browser limitations.
I've banished the use of IE from less than 1,000 yards from my laptop. I've used WiFi to escape from the clutches of Cat-5 cabling. I've converted from the almighty binary system to hexadecimal to octal to decimal system, but I've not been caught up for too long in any religion or cult. I'm sold on Agile although there are quite a few splinter groups. I'm love the smell of team spirit, and know about healthy competition. Always learning and absorbing the new, but not forsaking the old because I know current technology is built on the best of the past, just as the mighty terabyte must consider the lowly bit, even with checksums and such.

I've designed, depicted, and destroyed many a web page over the years. My experience with designers has been good. I'd rather have a mock-up or wire-frame than a blank page. I liken it to a web page version of having a painting-by-the-numbers of a Monet.

I struggle with stopping work. I love this stuff, but sometimes when debugging I need to be able to take a break from a vexing problem! I've never been a quitter. It just goes against my nature.

Finding a hair growing out of the side of my mashed potatoes bothers me big-time. People living in poverty, greed, and senseless wars and bother me. But besides that, missing semicolons at the end of JavaScript statements, non-indented code, forgetting to include "use strict" statements,css rules all on one line, using closing tag syntax (... />) with HTML5. Why do those things bother me? Because they are wrong. End of story.

Success is measured by meeting the goals and objectives of the stakeholders. That, and not having anyone lose an eye in the process.

I wish I was able to read users' minds instead of rely on them explaining what they want. I wish I could fly, too.

I approach it from the left after swinging by the coffee machine. It's not in my nature to begin by recalling where I left off from the day before. I don't have a way to turn it off and back on, so I'm often working on issues in my head after I leave work and making plans for getting back at it.

The trick is that I don't try to keep up with everything.That's why I specialize in keeping up primarily with web front-end technology. Used to be I'd subscribe to magazines and journals, but nowadays I read about stuff online. Plus I talk to other people I know in the field as well as people I don't know.

Keeping my skills current and not spreading myself too thin. That's why I've kept coming back to working on the front-end of websites. It's good to be well-rounded and understand the big picture and all the technologies involved in delivering solutions to customers, but no one has a deep understanding of all technologies involved with an end-to-end solution. I'm also passionate about doing the best possible job I can. If it's worth doing it is worth doing right.
